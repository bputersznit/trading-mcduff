Bookmap strategies are mainly **liquidity-and-order-flow strategies**. They use the heatmap, resting liquidity, market orders, absorption, pulling/stacking, and volume dots to infer where price is likely to react.

## 1. Liquidity Wall Rejection

A large bid/ask wall appears on the heatmap.

**Short idea:** price rises into a large ask wall, aggressive buyers hit it, but price cannot lift through.

Signal stack:

* Bright ask wall above price
* Large buy volume dots into the wall
* Price stalls
* Delta positive but price does not advance
* Ask liquidity remains or reloads

Trade: short against the wall.
Stop: above the wall.
Target: midpoint, VWAP, prior liquidity, or opposite bid wall.

## 2. Liquidity Wall Breakout

A large wall gets consumed instead of rejecting price.

**Long idea:** price attacks an ask wall and trades through it.

Signal stack:

* Bright ask wall
* Increasing buy volume
* Wall size decreases through trading, not just canceling
* Price lifts through wall
* Liquidity above disappears or becomes thin

Trade: long after break / retest.
Stop: below broken wall.
Target: next liquidity cluster.

## 3. Absorption Reversal

Aggressive market orders fail to move price.

**Bullish absorption:** sellers hit bid repeatedly, but price does not go lower.

Signal stack:

* Large red sell dots at/near low
* Bid holds
* Price stops making lower lows
* Delta very negative
* Sellers appear trapped
* Bid reloads

Trade: long after sellers fail.
Stop: below absorption low.
Target: VWAP / prior high / liquidity above.

## 4. Stop-Run Fade

Price spikes through a known level, triggers stops, then fails.

Example: price breaks prior high, prints big buy volume, then cannot continue.

Signal stack:

* Fast move through high/low
* Large aggressive volume
* Liquidity vanishes
* Immediate rejection back inside range
* Opposite-side aggression appears

Trade: fade the failed breakout.
Stop: beyond stop-run extreme.
Target: range midpoint or opposite side.

## 5. Pulling / Stacking Momentum

Book pressure changes before price moves.

**Bullish version:**

* Asks pull above price
* Bids stack below price
* Buy market orders increase
* Heatmap thins overhead

Trade: long with order-book pressure.
Stop: below stacked bid / recent swing.
Target: next ask wall.

## 6. Liquidity Vacuum Continuation

Price moves quickly through an area with little resting liquidity.

Signal stack:

* Thin heatmap ahead
* Strong market buys/sells
* No major opposing wall
* Fast tape speed
* Pulling on opposite side

Trade: continuation scalp.
Stop: behind last microstructure pivot.
Target: next major liquidity cluster.

## 7. Iceberg / Reload Detection

A level trades far more volume than visible liquidity suggests.

Signal stack:

* Same price keeps absorbing trades
* Visible size refreshes
* Large volume dots print repeatedly
* Price cannot pass through
* Hidden liquidity suspected

Trade depends on failure or break:

* If iceberg holds: fade into it.
* If iceberg breaks: trade breakout because trapped liquidity may unwind.

## 8. Opening Range Liquidity Strategy

Use Bookmap around the RTH open.

Key levels:

* 9:30–9:45 opening range
* Prior day high/low
* Overnight high/low
* VWAP
* Large heatmap walls

Best setups:

* OR high stop-run fade
* OR low stop-run fade
* OR breakout through wall
* Retest of broken liquidity wall
* Absorption at OR boundary

## 9. Whale-Hunting Strategy

Look for very large resting liquidity that shapes price behavior.

Signal stack:

* Large bid/ask wall persists
* Price repeatedly reacts there
* Wall moves intentionally
* Liquidity attracts price
* Aggressive orders either fail into it or consume it

Two modes:

* **Magnet mode:** price is drawn toward the wall.
* **Defense mode:** wall rejects price.

## 10. Confluence Strategy

The strongest Bookmap trades usually combine:

* Heatmap level
* Aggressive volume dots
* Delta behavior
* Pulling/stacking
* VWAP / OR / prior high-low
* MBO confirmation
* Volatility expansion
* Failed continuation or successful break

For your MNQ work, I would classify them into three families:

| Family        |            Best For | Core Signal                                         |
| ------------- | ------------------: | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Scalping      |      5–30 sec moves | pulling/stacking, vacuum, micro absorption          |
| Medium moves  |      1–10 min moves | wall rejection/break, OR interaction                |
| Whale hunting | larger displacement | persistent heatmap walls, iceberg, stop-run failure |

The best candidates for your ClickHouse/MBO pipeline are:

1. **Absorption failure**
2. **Wall rejection**
3. **Wall breakout**
4. **Stop-run fade**
5. **Liquidity vacuum continuation**
6. **Pulling/stacking pressure**
7. **Iceberg/reload detection**
8. **Opening range wall interaction**
